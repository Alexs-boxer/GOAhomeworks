Epipelagic Zone(Sunlight Zone) - 0m - 200 m

Also called the photic zone, where sunlight penetrates and supports photosynthesis.

Most fish - tuna , sardines , sharks and other

Tuna fish refers to several species of large, carnivorous, warm-blooded saltwater fish in the mackerel family, known for their speed and ability to maintain a body temperature higher than the surrounding water. Found in oceans worldwide, they are a commercially important food source, consumed fresh, canned, or raw.

Tuna

tuna - Tuna fish refers to several species of fast-swimming, predatory saltwater fish in the mackerel family, Scombridae, which are found in oceans worldwide. They are a popular food source, available fresh or canned, and known for their unique pink-to-dark-red, myoglobin-rich muscle meat.

Sardines

sardines - sardine, any of certain food fishes of the herring family, Clupeidae, especially members of the genera Sardina, Sardinops, and Sardinella; the name sardine can also refer to the common herring (Clupea harengus) and to other small herrings or herringlike

Shark

shark - Sharks are a group of elasmobranch cartilaginous fishes characterized by a ribless endoskeleton, dermal denticles, five to seven gill slits on each side, and pectoral fins that are not fused to the head. Modern sharks are classified within the division Selachii

Mesopelagic Zone (Twilight Zone) - 200m - 1000m

Light is faint or absent; not enough for photosynthesis.

Home to many bioluminescent fish like lanternfish, hatchetfish and bristlemouths.

lanternfish

Lanternfish - are small mesopelagic fish of the large family Myctophidae. One of two families in the order Myctophiformes, the Myctophidae are represented by 246 species in 33 genera, and are found in oceans worldwide.

hatchetfish

The freshwater hatchetfish are a family, Gasteropelecidae, of ray-finned fish from South and Central America. The common hatchetfish is the most popular member among fish keeping hobbyists.

bristlemouths

The Gonostomatidae are a family of mesopelagic marine fish, commonly named bristlemouths, lightfishes, or anglemouths. It is a relatively small family, containing only eight known genera and 32 species.

Bathypelagic Zone (Midnight Zone) - 1000m - 4000m

Completely dark, cold, and high-pressure environment.

include viperfish, gulper eels, dragonfish, and others with specialized adaptations.

viperfish

viperfish - is any species of marine fish in the genus Chauliodus. Viperfish are mostly found in the mesopelagic zone and are characterized by long, needle-like teeth and hinged lower jaws. A typical viperfish grows to lengths of 30 cm.

gulper eels

The pelican eel is a deep-sea eel. It is the only known member of the genus Eurypharynx and the family Eurypharyngidae. The species belongs to the "saccopharyngiforms", members of which were historically placed in their own order, but are now considered true eels in the order Anguilliformes.

dragonfish

Dragonfishes - are cunning predators. Although they are strong swimmers, they prefer to lie in wait and ambush unsuspecting fishes and crustaceans. Most have dark skin—pigmented with some of the blackest blacks known in nature—to stay camouflaged from their prey.
